How I run a UX Standalone Design Critique for a project

Summary: Step-by-step working process to systematically review the design project to find potential usability and experience problems. In this case study, I’ll share how I organized and conducted a standalone UX design critique session for a specific design of the project - Dermago - an asynchronous dermatology app.
The purpose of this critique was to review the user experience from the healthcare provider's perspective and identify pain points or opportunities for improvement.

Introduction

The asynchronous dermatology application is designed to streamline remote consultations between healthcare providers and patients. The core flow involves a patient submitting photos of their skin condition along with some basic information, and the dermatologist reviewing the case and providing a diagnosis and treatment plans. Ensuring an efficient and intuitive user experience for providers is crucial for faster diagnoses and treatment planning. This criqique focuses on evaluating usability issues and proposing design improvements to enhance workflow efficiency.

Selected Feature for Critique

Feature: Working on a Case - Diagnosis & Treatment Planning.

    The tasks involves:
  1. Overview of cases, status, urgent cases, timeline.
  2. Reviewing patient-submitted data - images, history, symptoms, prefered treatments.
  3. Entering a diagnosis and selecting treatment options.

Goals of the critique

  • Find out the UX Pain Points: Understand the pain points that dermatologists experience during the case review process, focusing on workflow and information architecture.
  • Improve Workflow Efficiency: Seek ways to streamline the case review process, reducing the number of clicks and interactions needed for dermatologists to get the information they need.
  • Enhance Communication Clarity: Evaluate how clearly and concisely the patient’s information, including images and history, is presented to healthcare providers.
  • Maintain Clinical Accuracy: Ensure that no changes would compromise the clinical integrity or accuracy of the dermatologist’s diagnosis.

Design Reviews

I organized a session with fellow UX designers and stakeholders. The agenda included: detailed examination of each feature, and inviting team members to provide input on identified issues and suggest improvements. This was done using tools like Figma, Miro, and Google Excel Sheet.

  1. Feature 1 Dashboard - Explored the provider dashboard, evaluating how intuitive it is for users to navigate.

    2. The diagnosis input area looks good, but I think there’s room for improvement in the treatment recommendation section. Providers typically follow specific protocols for common conditions like eczema or acne. It might be helpful to include preset templates for these common diagnoses.

  2. Feature 2 Patient Request Filter - Reviewed the workflow for managing patient consultation requests, including sorting, prioritizing, and tagging cases.

Findings The issues found were added to a list of usability issues on a shared digital sheet after a lively discussion.



Prioritizing Issues Once the critique was gone through all the features, we came to prioritize issues to tackle based on 2 criterias:

  • Impact on User ExperienceHow each issue affects the workflow of healthcare providers.
  • FeasibilityTechnical constraints and resource availability.

Results

To wrap up the critique session, I sent out the minute meeting and attached the essential documents so that the team will be easy to follow up. We had an effective session in terms of identify issues and come up with more holistic solutions, strengthening team dynamixs and promotiing shared ownership of the project. From that, it helps prioritize design decisions and align the team on key user experience goals, reducing confusion or misalignment on design intent.
